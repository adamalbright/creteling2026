Class 1Mon 20 July

Why does phonology care about morphological boundaries?

 Class 1 slides

Background readings (link to file on CreteLing website)

  • If you have no prior experience with Optimality Theory, McCarthy (2008) provides an accessible overview of some key features
  • Kenstowicz and Kisseberth (1977), chapter 2 surveys some of the ways in which phonological processes may refer to 'non-phonetic' information, including morphological structure

Further readings:

  • McCarthy, John.; and Alan Prince. 1986. Prosodic morphology. Technical report. New Brunswick, NJ: Rutgers University Center for Cognitive Science.
  • Peperkamp, Sharon. 1997. Prosodic Words. The Hague: Holland Academic Graphics.
  • Raffelsiefen, Renate. 2007. Morphological word structure in English and Swedish: the evidence from prosody. Proceedings of the Fifth Mediterranean Morphology Meeting, ed. by G. Booij, L. Ducceschi, B. Fradin, E. Guevara, A. Ralli, and S. Scalise, 209--268. Università degli Studi di Bologna.
